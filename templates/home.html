Project Management Life Cycle

The five essential phases of project management: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Monitoring & Controlling, and Closure.

Key Roles in Project Initiation

Initiation sets the stage with roles like the Sponsor, Manager, Stakeholders, Subject Matter Experts, and the Team for direction and alignment.

Project Management Plan

This phase defines how the project will be executed and monitored, including scope, risk, budget, timeline, and communications.

Project Execution

Project Execution brings the plan to life by managing deliverables through key domains like risk, time, quality, and communication.

Monitoring & Controlling

This stage ensures everything stays on track by continuously measuring performance and making real-time adjustments.

Steps Needed to Close a Project

Final project closure involves documentation, handover, evaluation, and celebration to confirm successful delivery.
